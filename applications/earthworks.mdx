## Overview

Earthworks monitoring tracks settlement, deformation, and stability of embankments, cuttings, fills, and earth structures during construction and long-term operation so you can verify design assumptions and manage risk to adjacent assets.

## Common monitoring goals

Earthworks projects typically focus on a small set of critical behaviors:

- **Settlement of embankments, fills, and earth structures** across wide areas where consolidation, loading, and staged construction can drive vertical movement.
- **Heave and rebound in excavations and cuttings** caused by unloading, groundwater changes, or changing construction conditions.
- **Lateral deformation and rotation of earth retaining structures and slopes** to identify developing instability and movement trends.
- **Ground movement adjacent to earthworks** that could affect nearby infrastructure, services, or supported ground.
- **Correlation with construction staging, loading, compaction, and rainfall** to understand how site activities and environmental conditions influence performance.
- **Continuous data for alarms and compliance** where trigger thresholds, reporting requirements, or risk controls apply.
- **Back-analysis and design refinement** using measured performance to improve interpretation and geotechnical design parameters.

## Relevant Osprey products

Use these Osprey systems to measure the most common earthworks behaviors.

<Columns cols="3">

<Card title="OTS" href="/ots/overview" icon="gauge">

OTS measures orientation and rotation along a line or curve to profile deformation and tilt of embankments, cuttings, and earth retaining structures.

</Card>

<Card title="ASP" href="/asp/overview" icon="droplets">

ASP measures distributed settlement along a nominally horizontal profile using the hydrostatic principle, useful for tracking settlement across wide embankment and fill areas.

</Card>

<Card title="IPX" href="/ipx/overview" icon="magnet">

IPX is a magnetic-field sensitive probe for automated monitoring of standard magnetic extensometer installations to measure vertical displacement at depth within embankments, fills, and foundation ground.

</Card>
