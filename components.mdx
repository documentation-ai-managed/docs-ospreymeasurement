The In-Place Extensometer is used to automate the monitoring of standard magnetic extensometer installations, typically comprising an access tube with end caps, datum magnet, magnetic targets, telescoping sections, and plate magnet.

## Magnetic Extensometer Components

### Access Casing

Access casing is grouted into a borehole to provide a void for installing the IPX sensors.

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="PVC Access Casing" default-open="true">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    A flush-coupled PVC pipe featuring a trapezoid coupling for rapid, secure installation down the borehole.

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - The In-Place Extensometer is optimised for 1 1/4" flush coupled PVC pipe:

    | Parameter      | Value                    |
    | -------------- | ------------------------ |
    | OD x ID        | 41.6mm x 31.6mm          |
    | Wall Thickness | 5.0mm                    |
    | PN             | 20                       |
    | SDR            | 8                        |
    | Thread Type    | Trapezoidal Flush Thread |

    - For installation in 1" (25mm ID) access pipe, for instance where retrofitting into existing magnetic extensometer installations, a slimline In-Place Extensometer must be used. The slimline IPX is suspended by a stainless steel wire from the top of the access tube.

    - Top caps will be replaced by the IPX top support. A well-cover is recommended to protect the installation from damage, ingress of debris or fill material, or intrusion by wildlife
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Inclinometer Casing" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    70mm or 85mm grooved casing, typically used for traversing or in-place inclinometer.

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - Where both settlement and inclination measurements are required, an <a href="https://www.geosense.com/products/ipi-x-in-place-inclinometer-extensometer/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">In-Place Inclinometer/ Extensometer (IPI-X)</a> should be used.&#x20;

    - Due to the larger diameter, consideration should be given to positioning the upper sensors to account for rod snaking.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Corrugrated Sleeves" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    A corrugated plastic pipe placed around the PVC access tube.&#x20;

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - Offers a relatively simple means of installing a large number of magnets within a borehole.

    - Good connection with the grout and ground.

    - Limited compressibility, so not suited to applications in soil with a high void ratio.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Telescoping Sections" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    Specialized in-line PVC sections consisting of an inner and outer pipe, used to accommodate compressive forces without stressing the primary casing when building through fill or using stiff grouts.

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    In most instances, the grout backfill in the borehole should be **sufficiently soft&#x20;**&#x73;o as it does not impart stress on the access tube as the ground settles. Where the access tube is likely to be under stress due to use of stiff grout, or where the access tube is extended through fill, protection in the form of an outer corrugated sleeve or telescoping sections should be used.&#x20;

    - **Bottom Supported Installations:**

      - Where telescoping sections are being used, sufficient clearance should be allowed within the well cover to accommodate the support rod stick-up as the installation consolidates

    - **Suspended Installations:**

      - Ensure there is sufficient clearance between the bottom of the GRP supporting rod and the bottom of the access pipe to accommodate settlement
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>

### Magnetic Targets

- The type and configuration of magnets used in magnetic targets may vary from one model to the next

- The IPX may require configuration for optimum performance with your magnetic target type

<Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
  The IPX operates by monitoring changes in a magnetic field. Therefore, the measurement magnets must be of magnetic type. The IPX will not work with non-magnetic systems such as rings used for induction-coil based systems.
</Callout>

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="Datum Magnet" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    A ring with embedded magnets installed near the base of the PVC pipe to serve as a reference point for the lowermost stable strata.

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - The datum magnet should be installed no less than 1m from the base of the PVC casing.&#x20;

    - Some designs call for two datum magnets. This configuration is not compatible with the IPX unless the magnets are at least 1m apart.&#x20;

    - The datum magnet is typically installed no less than 2m below the start of the identified stable strata.

    <Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
      The datum magnet **should not be fixed to the access tube**. In some circumstances, forces on the access tube may displace the access tube itself, and this movement should not be transferred to the datum magnet. The objective is for the datum magnet to represent the deep strata, not the casing.&#x20;
    </Callout>

    - In some soil conditions it is possible to use magnets with no sprung legs as the measurement targets, not just the datum.&#x20;
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="3 Legged Spider Magnets" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    Magnetic rings with three spring steel prongs that spring outwards to mechanically anchor into the walls of the borehole.&#x20;

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - Installed by pushing with a rod beyond the bottom of the drill casing

    - Ideal for where a large number of magnets are being installed in the same borehole.&#x20;

    - Requires a cased borehole for installation.&#x20;
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="6 Legged Spider Magnets" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    Magnetic rings with six prongs that spring outwards past the drill casing to mechanically anchor into the walls of the borehole.&#x20;

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - Excellent soft soil integration.&#x20;

    - Suitable for open hole installation where the ground conditions permit.

    - Not well suited to applications where the number of magnetic targets exceeds 8.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Magnetic Couplers for Corrugated Sleeves" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    Used in conjunction with corrugated outer sleeves. Can be couplers or externally mounted to a continuous corrugated sleeve.

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - Offers a relatively simple means of installing a large number of magnets within a borehole.

    - Good connection with the grout and ground.

    - Limited compressibility, so not suited to applications in soil with a high void ratio. &#x20;
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Plate Magnets" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    Plate magnets are ring magnets mounted on a 300mm disc. These are placed at the ground surface and used to monitor original ground level, or to placed within the fill itself to add measurement points within rising fill.

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - Plate magnets can be used as a surface reference for survey where a deep datum cannot be achieved

    <Callout kind="danger" collapsed="false">
      Where extensometers are being extended through fill, protection of the access pipe is essential. This can be achieved using telescoping joints or a corrugated sleeve around the access pipe.
    </Callout>
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>

### Reed Switch Probe

A reed switch probe is required for initial survey/confirmation of magnet positions.

The offset between the tape measurement gradations and the very tip of the probe should be known to allow the depth of the base of the access pipe to be measured. This is essential for correct positioning of the IPX in bottom supported installations

## In-Place Extensometer Components

An IPX system comprises of one or more IPX sensors, a means of supporting or suspending the sensors within the access tube, and a cable for communicating with the sensors.&#x20;

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="IPX Sensors" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    Magnetic-field sensitive probes used to measure changes in position of a magnetic field. Construct is a 710mm long stainless steel tube with end caps for fixing position along a GRP rod or stainless steel wire.&#x20;

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - The IPX is available as a standard, rod mounted system, or a slimline, wire mounted option

    - Slimline must be used where the access pipe ID is less than 30mm
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Cabling & Communication" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    A four-core cable that connects each sensor to the adjacent one, forming an RS-485 serial bus linked to a data logger.

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - Excess cable between sensors is recommended to allow technicians to adjust the positions of the sensors on site. This is option is not available with slimline IPX.&#x20;

    - Consider the length of cable from the top of the borehole required to reach the data logger position, or where the extensometer will be extended through fill.

    - In-line submersible connectors are available for rapid connect/ disconnect on site; ideal where sections of casing and/ or sensors will be added/ removed as part of the construction process.&#x20;

      <Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
        The cable has a reduced quantity of outer jacket material to support tight bend radius, and therefore is vulnerable to pinch and puncture damage. Significant precautions should be made to protect the cable during installation. The cable will fail if subject to greater than 50N of tensile load.
      </Callout>
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Top Support" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    &#x20;A stainless steel stepped cap with grub screws for mounting on GRP rod or suspension kit.

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - The stainless-steel top support  is used to steady the support rod at the top of the installation, or in top supported installations, suspend the string of IPX sensors.

    <Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
      OMS plastic coated GRP rod and grub screws tightened to a torque of 0.5Nm has been tested to a load of 40kg. This will allow suspension of 35 IPX sensors and 100m of rod. Where more sensors are installed in a top supported configuration, we recommend the use of a split shaft collar for additional support.
    </Callout>

    - The top support replaces the PVC top cap typically supplied with a magnetic extensometer installation.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Bottom Support" default-open="true">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    A tapered stainless steel collar fixed to the support rod using two grub screws.&#x20;

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    - The bottom support is used to centre and protect the support rod during installation and when sitting on the bottom of the access tube.&#x20;

    - It should be used in all cases, regardless of whether the installation is top supported or bottom supported.
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="Plastic Coated GRP Rod" default-open="false">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    A 6mm plastic coated GRP rod, used to provide fixed positions for the IPX, whether supported from the bottom or suspended from the top.

    **<u>Considerations: </u>**

    - Available with a reel, or without for refills. The reel is essential for a safe, controlled installation.&#x20;

      <Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
        Where GRP rod is being sourced locally, the below features should be specifically requested. Unsatisfactory results have been observed where poor quality GRP rod has been used.
      </Callout>

    * Must be plastic coated – this provides better fixing for grub screws, reduces risk of damaging the rod and minimizes risk of injury during installation

    * Outer diameter: **6mm**

    - 4mm diameter fiberglass core with high resin content for high tensile and compressive strength

    - 1mm thick polypropylene sheath for good longevity in submersed conditions
  </Expandable>

  <Expandable title="IPX Suspension Kit" default-open="true">
    **<u>Description:</u>**

    The IPX suspension kit is supplied with slimline IPX specifically, and comprises a top support with wire suspension collet, stainless steel wire, and suspension weight.

    **<u>Considerations:</u>**

    The slimline IPX and suspension kit are ideal for retro-fitting automated monitoring in existing 1" magnetic extensometer installations.&#x20;

    - A dummy probe should be used to confirm traversability ahead of purchasing the system.&#x20;

    - Magnetic compatibility varies and should be confirmed with Osprey ahead of deployment.&#x20;

    In-line connectors are used to allow quick and easy on-site connection between IPXs or at the top of the IPX string. Connectors are recommended where an IPX string is expected to be extended or shortened after installation.

## Data Logger

The IPX operates on commands sent and received over an RS-485 serial bus. It has both an ASCII command set and a ModBUS RTU interface.

To interact with the IPX, a data logger must be:

1. **Pre-configured** to be compatible with Osprey Measurement Systems' IPX

   **OR**

2. **Configurable** such that the delay, TX and RX commands can be user defined

   **AND**

3. **Able to provide** sufficient power and voltage to power the required number of IPX sensors

####

<Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
  To confirm a data logging system's compatibility with the IPX, or for assistance with integrating the IPX with your preferred monitoring platform, please contact Osprey Measurement Systems' support team.
</Callout>

The IPX can be configured and monitored using a laptop, field PC or mobile device using a USB serial port interface.

### Power Requirements

The IPX will function between **4 to 16V**. An IPX that has been exposed to non-transitory voltages above 24V should be removed from service.

#### Boot Power Consumption

On powerup, IPX sensors are sequentially booted up one at a time starting with the closest sensor to the power source/data logger. This process takes \~100ms per sensor on the string.

#### Measurement Power Consumption

Polling of the IPX string results in sequential power use by each IPX.

#### Idle Power Consumption

Each IPX draws **4mA at 12V** in idle.
