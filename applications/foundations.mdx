## Overview

Foundation monitoring tracks settlement, heave, and tilt or rotation of foundation elements during construction and long-term operation so you can verify design assumptions and protect adjacent structures from ground movement.

## Common monitoring goals

Foundation projects typically focus on a small set of critical behaviors:

- **Settlement of shallow and deep foundation elements** across pads, rafts, piles, and pile groups.
- **Heave beneath and around foundations** caused by excavation, unloading, groundwater changes, or other construction effects.
- **Tilt and rotation of pads, piles, and rafts** to identify differential movement and developing deformation.
- **Ground movement adjacent to foundations** that could affect nearby structures, services, or retained ground.
- **Correlation with construction staging, loading, and dewatering** to understand how activities influence foundation response.
- **Continuous data for alarms and compliance** where trigger levels, reporting requirements, or risk controls apply.
- **Back-analysis and design refinement** using measured performance to improve interpretation and future design decisions.

## Relevant Osprey products

Use these Osprey systems to measure the most common foundation behaviors.

<Columns cols="3">

<Card title="OTS" href="/ots/overview" icon="gauge">

OTS measures orientation and rotation along a line or curve to profile tilt and deformation of foundation elements, piles, and retaining structures.

</Card>

<Card title="ASP" href="/asp/overview" icon="droplets">

ASP measures distributed settlement along a nominally horizontal profile using the hydrostatic principle, useful for tracking settlement beneath and around foundation footprints.

</Card>

<Card title="IPX" href="/ipx/overview" icon="magnet">

IPX is a magnetic-field sensitive probe for automated monitoring of standard magnetic extensometer installations to measure vertical displacement at depth beneath foundations, deep foundation elements, and pile tips.

</Card>
