## Overview

Dam monitoring tracks deformation, settlement, and structural behavior of dam embankments, concrete sections, spillways, and appurtenant structures during construction, first filling, and long-term operation so you can verify design assumptions, detect developing issues early, and support safe operation.

## Common monitoring goals

Dam projects typically focus on a small set of critical behaviors:

- **Settlement and consolidation of embankment dams** across the crest and downstream shoulder where staged construction and reservoir loading drive vertical movement.
- **Deformation and rotation of concrete dam sections** including monolith tilt, joint movement, and structural response to hydrostatic loading.
- **Internal deformation and shear within embankments** to identify developing slip surfaces or zones of distress.
- **Spillway and appurtenant structure movement** including settlement, tilt, and differential displacement relative to the main dam body.
- **Correlation with reservoir level, seepage, rainfall, and temperature** to understand how environmental and operational conditions influence dam behavior.
- **Continuous data for alarm thresholds and compliance** where regulatory requirements, trigger levels, or risk controls apply.
- **Back-analysis and design refinement** using measured performance to improve interpretation and future dam design decisions.

## Relevant Osprey products

Use these Osprey systems to measure the most common dam behaviors.

<Columns cols="3">

<Card title="OTS" href="/ots/overview" icon="gauge">

OTS measures orientation and rotation along a line or curve to profile deformation and tilt of dam walls, concrete sections, and embankment slopes.

</Card>

<Card title="ASP" href="/asp/overview" icon="droplets">

ASP measures distributed settlement along a nominally horizontal profile using the hydrostatic principle, useful for tracking settlement across dam crests and broad embankment surfaces.

</Card>

<Card title="IPX" href="/ipx/overview" icon="magnet">

IPX is a magnetic-field sensitive probe for automated monitoring of standard magnetic extensometer installations to measure vertical displacement at depth within embankment dams and foundation ground.

</Card>
