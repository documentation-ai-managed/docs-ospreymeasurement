## Prerequisites

Before starting installation, ensure you have the necessary tools and site conditions.

<Callout kind="info" title="Required Tools and Materials">
- Osprey Tilt String (OTS) or In-Place Extensometer (IPX) sensor cable
- Mounting brackets or concrete embed kits
- Serial bus connectors and termination resistors
- Cable protection conduits (PVC or HDPE)
- Multimeter for continuity testing
- Waterproof sealant and zip ties
- Data logger compatible with RS-485 serial bus
</Callout>

Verify site conditions:
- Temperature between `-40°C` and `+85°C`
- No excessive vibration beyond sensor specs
- Clear access for trenching or embedding

## Site Assessment and Preparation

Perform a thorough site survey to ensure optimal sensor placement.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Survey the Area" icon="map">
    Identify mounting points along the asset (e.g., wall, pipeline). Mark positions every `1-5m` based on required resolution.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prepare Surface" icon="tools">
    Clean surfaces of debris, oil, or loose material. For trenches, excavate to `0.5m` depth.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test Ground Conditions" icon="activity">
    Check soil stability and moisture levels. Use a geotechnical probe if embedding in soil.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">
  Document all measurements with photos and GPS coordinates for future reference.
</Callout>

## Mounting Techniques

Choose the appropriate method based on your asset type. Follow these platform-specific instructions.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Concrete Embed (Diaphragm Wall)" icon="hard-drive">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Position Sensors" icon="pin">
        Lay the OTS cable in the wall formwork at desired depths.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Secure Cable" icon="lock">
        Use embed clips spaced `0.5m` apart to fix the cable.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Pour Concrete" icon="droplets">
        Ensure cable remains straight during pour. Cure for `48 hours`.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    ```mermaid
    graph TD
        A[Formwork] --> B[Place OTS Cable]
        B --> C[Secure with Clips]
        C --> D[Pour Concrete]
        D --> E[Sensor Embedded]
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Trench Burial (Pipeline)" icon="digging">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Dig Trench" icon="shovel">
        Excavate `0.3m` wide by `0.6m` deep along pipeline route.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Lay Cable" icon="git-branch">
        Place OTS parallel to pipeline, minimum `0.1m` separation.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Backfill" icon="package">
        Cover with sand layer, then native soil. Compact in `0.15m` lifts.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Surface Mount (Railway Wall)" icon="monitor">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Attach Brackets" icon="settings">
        Drill and bolt mounting brackets to wall every `2m`.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Route Cable" icon="zap">
        Secure OTS to brackets with ties. Avoid sharp bends (`<30cm` radius).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Protect Exposed Sections" icon="shield">
        Use conduit over vulnerable areas.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Serial Bus Connections

Connect multiple sensors (up to `150`) in a daisy-chain serial bus using RS-485 protocol.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Terminate Ends" icon="plug">
    Add `120Ω` termination resistors at both cable ends.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wire Connections" icon="network">
    Connect A+ to A+, B- to B-, GND to GND at each junction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect to Logger" icon="database">
    Route bus to data logger. Use shielded cable for runs `>50m`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Continuity" icon="check-circle">
    Use multimeter: Resistance between A/B should be `~120Ω`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<CodeGroup tabs="Python,JavaScript">
  ```python
  import serial

  ser = serial.Serial('/dev/ttyUSB0', 9600, timeout=1)
  ser.write(b'SENSOR_QUERY\n')
  data = ser.read(1024)
  print(data.decode())  # e.g., "TILT:0.05deg,0.02deg"
  ```
  ```javascript
  const SerialPort = require('serialport');
  const port = new SerialPort({ path: '/dev/ttyUSB0', baudRate: 9600 });
  port.write('SENSOR_QUERY\n');
  port.on('data', (data) => {
    console.log(data.toString());  // e.g., "TILT:0.05deg,0.02deg"
  });
  ```
</CodeGroup>

## Environmental Protection and Embedding

Protect sensors from harsh conditions for long-term reliability.

<Expandable title="Advanced Protection Methods" default-open="false">
  - **Conduit Use**: Sleeve cables in `IP68` rated PVC for burial.
  - **Sealant Application**: Apply silicone around junctions (`>5mm` bead).
  - **Vibration Damping**: Add foam padding under surface mounts.
  - **Lightning Protection**: Install surge arrestors at logger entry.

  | Method          | Environment     | Protection Level |
  |-----------------|-----------------|------------------|
  | PVC Conduit     | Buried          | High             |
  | Silicone Seal   | Junctions       | Medium           |
  | Surge Arrester  | Exposed Bus     | High             |
</Expandable>
