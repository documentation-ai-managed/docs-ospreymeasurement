## Adjusting the height after installation

It is a common requirement to adjust the height of the extensometer to accommodate construction progress. 

Core Principles

-          The magnets are intended to represent the soil strata in which they are installed. Fixing the datum magnet to the access tube will mean the magnet will represent the access tube, not the soil strata.

-          The access tube should be delaminated from the grout. Use a soft, high bentonite grout and flush coupled casing to ensure the grout does not put stress on the access tube.

-          The system relies on a fixed position as a reference, typically a deep datum. Where this cannot be achieved due to drilling constraints or the source of movement being below the installation, control must be brought to the top of the installation, via manual or automatic survey methods, GNSS, or settlement profiler.

-          The distance between the IPX sensors is assumed to be fixed. This is achieved either by supporting the sensors from the bottom, or suspending them from the top. If the sensor string are partially suspended but resting on the base, any compression of the access tube will result in changing distances between sensors and hence diminished measurement reliability.
